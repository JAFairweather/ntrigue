NTRIGUE

A phones-only party game of secrets, dilemmas, and blackmail. Four friends around a table; everyone answers a spicy prompt privately; pairwise prisoner's-dilemma exchanges decide who sees what; the finale is a blackmail economy where everything you've collected is leverage.

Design law: you can revoke a secret, but you can't un-tell it.

The plumbing

There is no game server. Your table runs peer-to-peer over public nostr relays using NIP-DA Scoped Data Grants: every answer is a kind-30440 encrypted scope held only by its author, and a trade delivers a kind-440 gift-wrapped grant of that scope's key directly to the counterpart. Relays store ciphertext; they never see an answer's plaintext or who traded with whom. The host phone publishes only public game state — who answered, pairings, choices, scores — never secret contents.
